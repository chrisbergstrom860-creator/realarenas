🏟 Scoring

How points work

Every point on Arenas comes from an activity someone logged. This page is the entire scoring system — no hidden multipliers, no boosts.

Two ways to earn points

Every sport on Arenas scores one of two ways. There is no third way, and no way to earn points without logging an activity.

Per kilometre

Distance sports

{{KM_SPORTS_SENTENCE}} score points for every kilometre you log. The further you go, the more you earn.

Per session

Everything else

Every other sport earns a fixed number of points per session. One logged activity is one session, whether it lasted 20 minutes or 4 hours.

Two worked examples

Example 1 — a week of running

You log three runs: 8 km on Tuesday, 5 km on Thursday, and 10 mi on Sunday.

8 km × {{RUN_RATE}} = {{EX1_A}} pts
5 km × {{RUN_RATE}} = {{EX1_B}} pts
10 mi = 16.09 km (miles × 1.609)  →  16.09 km × {{RUN_RATE}} = {{EX1_C}} pts Week total: {{EX1_A}} + {{EX1_B}} + {{EX1_C}} = {{EX1_RAW}}  →  {{EX1_TOTAL}} pts

Totals are rounded once, at the end — never per activity.

Example 2 — a mixed week

You log a 25 km ride, one climbing session, and one yoga session.

25 km × {{RIDE_RATE}} = {{EX2_A}} pts
Climbing session = {{CLIMB_RATE}} pts
Yoga session = {{YOGA_RATE}} pts Week total: {{EX2_A}} + {{CLIMB_RATE}} + {{YOGA_RATE}} = {{EX2_TOTAL}} pts

Session sports pay the same whether the session was easy or brutal — only you know the difference.

The ground rules

  • Points come from logged activities — nothing else. No sign-up bonuses, no streak multipliers. Delete an activity and its points go with it.
  • Distance is unit-aware. Log a run as "10 mi" and it counts as 16.1 km. Metres work too — "2,000 m" counts as 2 km. No unit means kilometres.
  • Weekly leaderboards start Monday at 00:00 in your own timezone. Monthly figures use calendar months. All-time means all time.
  • Values are set for rough effort-parity across sports. A climbing session is worth about the same as a 5 km run. These are judgment calls, honestly made — not sports science.
  • Two small edge cases, for completeness: a distance-sport activity logged without a distance earns twice its per-km rate as a flat credit, and an activity in a sport Arenas no longer recognises earns a flat 20 points.
