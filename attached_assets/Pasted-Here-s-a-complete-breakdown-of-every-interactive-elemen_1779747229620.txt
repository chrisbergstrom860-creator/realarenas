Here's a complete breakdown of every interactive element and design decision:
Filter bar — two rows, multiple filter types working together
Sport filters (Running, Cycling, Climbing, Swimming, Triathlon) toggle independently — turning one off removes those events from the grid live. "All sports" resets all. When and distance filters are mutually exclusive — selecting "Next 3 months" deselects "This month". Type filters (5K, 10K, Half marathon, etc.) toggle independently. The "✦ AI matched only" pill in amber is a different filter type — it hides any event with an AI match score below 80%, filtering to events you're actually ready for. The count in the top right updates live showing how many events are visible after all filters apply.
Three view modes — Grid (default 2-column), Map, and List. Grid switches to single column in list mode. Map view reveals the interactive pin map with 7 colour-coded pins across London — each sport has its own colour (yellow for featured/going, orange for running, blue for cycling, purple for climbing, teal for swimming). Clicking any pin fires a toast with the event name.
Featured event banner — the London Half Marathon sits above the grid with a large 94% AI match score badge (green background, its own visual card), clubmate avatar strip naming Sofia, Yuki, and Alena specifically, and two CTAs. The yellow RSVP button turns green with "You're going!" on click and notifies the coach. "View details" opens the full event modal.
Event cards — each has a sport-coloured date box, title, location with distance, tag pills (sport, fee, AI match score, clubmate count), and a social row showing the first two clubmate avatars with a "+N" overflow. RSVP buttons on all cards update to green "✓ Going" on click. Cards that match no active filter disappear from the grid and the border styling adjusts automatically to avoid orphaned right borders on the last visible card in each row.
Event detail modal — clicking any card or the "View details" button opens a full-detail modal. Seven events have unique content: London Half has a full coach note (code HRC2026, meet at 6:30 AM, club vest), attendee strip with named clubmates, AI projection of 1:50–1:52 finish time, and three RSVP buttons (Going / Maybe / Can't make it) that update state independently. Victoria Park Parkrun has an AI warning to keep the pace controlled (zone 2–3) rather than race it two weeks before the half. Richmond Sportive has AI context on treating it as endurance base rather than a race. Each event's modal date box adopts the sport colour theme.
Search bar — typing in the topbar search filters the events grid in real time by matching against all visible text in each card, including titles, locations, and tag labels.
Load more — adds two more event cards (Regent's Park 5K and Crystal Palace Triathlon) with proper animation delays, then replaces itself with "All nearby events loaded".
Sidebar — three cards working together: Your RSVPs shows two confirmed (London Half, Parkrun) and one pending (Hackney Half) with a deadline warning. The "Near you this month" list shows five events sorted by proximity with distance labels. The Hackney RC club events list colour-codes status — green dots for confirmed, orange for pending, and the Hackney Half row is highlighted in yellow with an inline RSVP button and "8 days left" deadline pressure. The AI recommendation card at the top connects everything — it tells Jamie exactly why the London Half is the right choice in his specific situation (1:54 PB, 62km/week training, 34 clubmates going).
