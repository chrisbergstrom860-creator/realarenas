Here's a complete breakdown of everything interactive and every design decision:
Filter system — two rows, multiple independent dimensions
Show filter (All / Following / Followers / Hackney RC / Recommended) is mutually exclusive — selecting one shows a different subset. Level filter (All / Advanced / Intermediate / Beginner) is also mutually exclusive. Location (Near me / London / UK / Global) and Activity (Active this week / month / All time) each work the same way. All filters fire a toast confirming the change. The search bar in the topbar filters the entire page in real time — it hides the Recommended strip and Nearby section during a search to surface only matching cards.
Recommended strip — four cards, each with an explicit reason
Every card shows why Arenas is surfacing that athlete. Priya: "Hackney RC · rank #4 · same pace bracket as you (4:20–4:30/km)." Marco: "Similar FTP to you (241W vs your 241W) · both targeting Stelvio climbs." Omar: "0.6 mi from you · trains the same Hackney Marshes routes on Tuesdays." Kate: "V5 climber — you're projecting V6. Following her beta could help your send." These reasons are only possible because Arenas has pace data, power data, GPS route data, and grade data combined. The Follow button on each card updates state on click.
Sport tab bar — clicking Running filters the grid to show only runners and updates the athlete count. Clicking Climbing shows only climbers with climbing-specific stats (grade, sessions/week). The grid border logic adjusts automatically so there's never an orphaned right border on the last visible card in a row.
Athlete cards — each one has a sport-coloured avatar, sport/level/club/proximity pills, three contextual stats (runners show km and streak, cyclists show FTP and W/kg, swimmers show pace and format), and an 8-week sparkline bar chart colour-coded by sport (orange for running, blue for cycling, purple for climbing, teal for swimming). Grey bars show lower-volume weeks or rest. The Follow button toggles between yellow (Follow) and light red on hover when already following.
Grid / List toggle — switching to list view collapses the sparkline bars and rearranges each card into a horizontal single-row layout. The grid immediately re-renders without reload.
Athlete profile modal — clicking any card opens a full profile modal with a dark hero section (same treatment as the club dashboard and for clubs page), showing name, handle, sport/level/club tags with sport-coloured backgrounds, and two action buttons (Follow and Message). Below that: four stats in a grid, a bio paragraph, and the athlete's most recent activity card with stats and AI coaching analysis. The Follow button in the modal syncs independently. Ten athletes have complete unique data including bios, recent activities, and AI insights.
Nearby section — a 2-column grid below the main athlete grid, sorted by distance from Jamie's location. Each card shows distance first (monospace, prominent), then avatar, name, sport/level/distance context, club badge if applicable, and a Follow button. Alena is 0.4 mi, Omar is 0.6 mi, Tom is 1.2 mi, Jake is 2.8 mi, Rachel is 3.4 mi, Yuki N. is 3.2 mi.
Load more — adds two more athlete cards (Jamie O. and Kai W.) with proper sport sparklines and replaces the button with a "All athletes loaded" message.
Right sidebar — three sections: People you follow (5 athletes with their current streak and weekly stats, each clickable to open the profile modal), Your network (following/follower counts with a delta showing 4 new followers this week), sport breakdown bar chart of your network (63 runners, 16 cyclists, 6 climbers, 4 swimmers), and Clubs to discover (South London CC, East End Climbers, London Tri Club) with Join buttons that fire confirmation toasts.
