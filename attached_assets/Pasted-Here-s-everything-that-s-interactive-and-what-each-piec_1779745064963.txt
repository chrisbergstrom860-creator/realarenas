Here's everything that's interactive and what each piece does:
Filter bar — six filter pills (All, Running, Cycling, Climbing, Clubs only, AI insights) each live-filter the feed on click. Cards that don't match the filter animate out instantly, and a toast confirms how many posts remain. The sort dropdown is also wired and triggers a toast — in production this would re-sort the feed order.
Card 1 — AI coaching nudge sits at the very top and is the highest-priority card type. It references Jamie's actual data (42% load spike, HR drift in yesterday's ride, specific target of 150 bpm), connects to a named teammate (Yuki had the same issue), and ties to Jamie's stated goal (London Marathon). Three actions: mark rest day, view load data, or dismiss. Clicking dismiss animates the card out with a slide-up fade.
Cards 2, 4, 8, 9, 11 — Activity cards each have the sport colour-coded header tag, a row of metric stat blocks with monospace numbers, a description, and a footer with kudos (toggleable — count updates live), comments, and share. Cards for Sofia and Sofia R. have the AI coach panel open inline. Cards for Marco and Yuki have the AI coach collapsed with a "✦ AI coach" button to open it. Kudos toggle the heart count up and down on every card independently.
Card 3 — Club update (event RSVP) has the club banner stripe at the top, a bi-colour RSVP progress bar (green for going, orange for pending), status pills, and inline RSVP buttons. Clicking "I'm going" changes the button to green with a disabled state and fires a toast confirming Rachel is notified.
Card 5 — Achievement card uses a yellow gradient banner and a medal avatar. It has a different visual treatment to celebration moments — more celebratory and distinct from a regular activity post.
Card 6 — Challenge progress shows Jamie's current position in the 100km May Run with a three-column mini leaderboard. Jamie's column is highlighted in yellow. The "Log a run" button opens the log activity modal.
Card 7 — Event discovery has a blue recommendation banner explaining why it was surfaced ("2.4km away · matches your sports"), the event details, the coach note in yellow, and RSVP buttons.
Card 10 — Club update (challenge nudge) shows which members are at risk of breaking their streak with a "Nudge teammates" button that fires a toast.
Sidebar — your stats block with this week's km and club rank, the leaderboard with rank trend arrows, the 7-day streak dot grid (one missed day is shown in red), a "who to follow" list with follow/unfollow toggle buttons that update their own state, quick actions that open relevant modals, and a trending topics section with post counts.
Three modals: Comments shows a threaded conversation and a reply input. Log activity has sport type buttons, form fields for name/distance/duration/date, and a yellow callout explaining AI coaching will run automatically. Notifications shows five items, two with inline action buttons (RSVP and kudos) that work without closing the modal.
